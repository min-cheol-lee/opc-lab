Why OPC matters?

In semiconductor industry photolithography, light diffraction and scattering distort tiny mask shapes, so the printed pattern can miss transistor targets. OPC pre-corrects the mask so wafer prints land on target dimensions for better yield and performance.

Why a simple square contact can print wrong

Even with a square opening on the mask, optical diffraction and scattering blur the aerial image, so corners round off and the print shifts from the intended target.
